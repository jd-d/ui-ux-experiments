Trigger Monitor glossary

A reference for the cycle stage labels, dashboard callouts, and scoring fields that appear across the site.

Cycle position

The homepage hero distills the 18.6-year land cycle into a single stage label (for example, “Late Expansion”) and a stress qualifier such as “Fragility heightened.” That snapshot is curated by site maintainers using the live leaderboard and latest briefings produced from the ingestion script, then stamped with an update note (currently “Baseline view updated 24 September 2025”).

Stages advance when multiple clusters in the event registry shift phases or scores. Maintainers mark “Late Expansion” when macro growth is still running, yet the leaderboard is dominated by elevated or critical risks. “Fragility heightened” flags that overlapping dashboard pillars are flashing High stress or Elevated, signalling that cracks are spreading across domains even before an outright break.

Current inputs
  • Leaderboard events: Scores and phases are sourced from data/leaderboard.json, which is rebuilt by scripts/ingest_llm_packet.py on each packet ingest.
  • Latest briefings: The hero links point to the freshest HTML briefings referenced in data/latest_briefings.json.

Trigger dashboard states

The four dashboard cards map to the macro pillars tracked in every research cycle. Each state is assigned from the underlying briefs cited in the card and signals the live level of stress:

  • High stress: Observable dislocations already impair liquidity or funding (e.g., shipping outages or stalled dealer balance sheets).
  • Elevated: Core indicators are deteriorating quickly, but market functioning remains intact.
  • On watch: Early deterioration that warrants closer monitoring, usually led by fiscal or policy slippage.
  • Moderate: Mixed signals—some speculative excess persists, yet positioning or sentiment still allows for further upside.

When the ingestion pipeline refreshes posts, the dashboard bullets are replaced with the most recent briefs for that pillar. Manual revisions should be paired with an entry in the transparency log below.

Trigger risk badges

Metadata ribbons on each briefing use colour-coded "Trigger risk" badges to signal how far an event has progressed along the stress curve. The badge text mirrors the scoring phase and provides a quick shorthand before readers dive into the narrative detail.

Watch
Early deterioration is visible, but market plumbing and policy backstops are still holding. Analysts monitor tripwires to see whether the issue spreads or resolves without intervention.
Elevated
Stress is now broad-based: price signals, positioning, or policy responses are flashing amber. Follow-up briefs probe second-order contagion channels and the probability of a move into outright crisis.
Critical
Market function is impaired or institutional support is being overwhelmed. Capital controls, defaults, or emergency policy actions are either underway or imminent, demanding immediate playbook responses.

Event taxonomy

Each briefing inherits a structured classification from the ingest pipeline. Event types describe the mechanism of stress, while clusters group related risks so cross-cutting signals remain linked over time.

Event types

Banking strain
Runs, liquidity gaps, or capital shortfalls across depository lenders.
Climate disruption
Extreme weather or climate-driven shocks that curtail production, mobility, or critical services.
CRE credit crunch
Funding stress specific to commercial real estate exposures, especially for lenders rolling short-dated debt.
Grid stress
Power-generation or transmission bottlenecks that threaten rolling outages or forced curtailment.
Infrastructure risk (alias: Infrastructure)
Structural failures in transport, energy, or communications networks with systemic spillovers.
Local debt crisis
Fiscal stress concentrated in municipal or provincial balance sheets, often via opaque financing vehicles.
Market tail-risk
Option structures, leverage, or positioning that could trigger non-linear price moves under stress.
Port operational failure
Physical damage, labour disputes, or chokepoint shutdowns that halt cargo throughput.
Property bust
Broad-based real estate price declines undermining household wealth or developer solvency.
Sovereign fiscal stress
Chronic deficits or debt-servicing burdens pushing governments toward market exclusion or austerity.
Sovereign outlook change
Rating agencies revising forward guidance on a sovereign, signalling the next likely move in the formal rating.
Sovereign rating watch
A formal watch status preceding an imminent upgrade or downgrade within the agency review window.
Trade slowdown
Persistent drops in shipping volumes, export orders, or customs receipts.
Typhoon disruption
Severe storms with cyclone characteristics that impair coastal or inland infrastructure.
Zoonosis
Animal-to-human disease transmission events with pandemic potential.

Event clusters

AI POWER
High-power compute infrastructure, from data centres to transformer supply chains, where outages can ripple through the AI buildout.
Agritech
Large-scale agricultural technology projects, including energy-intensive irrigation and processing hubs that strain rural infrastructure.
CHINA CREDIT
China’s credit complex, including property developers, local government vehicles, and bank balance sheets.
EURO SOV
Euro-area sovereign risk, especially fiscal divergence between core and periphery issuers.
H5N1
Avian influenza outbreaks and mutation risk at the animal-human interface.
ODTE
Zero-day and very short-dated options flows that can whip markets when liquidity thins.
SHIPPING
Maritime logistics corridors, covering ports, canals, and bulk carrier availability.
US CRE
United States commercial real estate, spanning office, retail, and income-producing multifamily credit.

Core scoring fields

Event updates in the JSON packet feed a canonical registry (data/events.json). That registry powers the leaderboard, post metadata, and latest-briefing feed. Key fields behave as follows:

Score
Numeric value clamped between 0 and 100. Ingested scores of 50 represent baseline vigilance, and stale events lose three points per day once they are more than seven days old.
Phase
Discrete state (watch, elevated, or critical) that controls colour coding and determines whether decay applies.
Confidence
Subjective strength of evidence (high, medium, low) carried into post metadata.
Indicators
Quantitative hooks (e.g., spreads, load factors) that justify the phase or score.
Tripwires
Discrete developments that would force a score or phase reassessment.
Rationale
Short-form explanations supporting the current assessment. New entries are deduplicated against prior updates to preserve provenance.
Sources
Canonical URLs kept to four per leaderboard row. During ingest, URLs are normalised to avoid duplicates.
History
Time series of score changes. Each ingest appends a new entry so trends are visible to analysts reviewing the registry.
Fingerprint
Stable SHA-256 hash derived from cluster, event type, entities, geography, instruments, mechanism, and canonical source. It guarantees continuity when an event is revisited.
