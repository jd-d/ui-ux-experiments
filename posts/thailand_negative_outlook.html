Thailand’s Credit Outlook Cut to Negative

Trigger risk: Watch Sovereign Outlook Change EURO SOV Confidence: Medium Score: 65

What changed

Fitch revised Thailand’s long‑term credit outlook from "stable" to "negative", while affirming its BBB+ rating, citing pressure on public finances and governance risks.

Implications

  • Risk of full downgrade looms, potentially triggering bond selloffs.
  • Investor confidence in ASEAN sovereigns could shift toward risk aversion.

Watch next

  • Further rating actions by agencies
  • Yield spreads widening in Malaysian, Philippine, Indonesian debt
  • Moves in ASEAN FX and bond flows
Sources: Fitch; Reuters
